Our Story

About the Gurukulam

A living lineage of Kalaripayattu — preserved through dedication, discipline, and devotion.

Head Master

Brejesh S. Gurukkal

Trained under the revered lineage of the MVG CVN tradition, Brejesh S. Gurukkal has dedicated his life to the authentic preservation and propagation of Kalaripayattu. After dedicated service in the Kerala Police — where he trained cadets in Yoga and Kalaripayattu — he took voluntary retirement in September 2024 to fully commit to this sacred mission.

Today, he leads the Marari Gurukulam with a vision to keep this ancient knowledge alive — not just as combat, but as a complete system for healing, movement, and spiritual awakening.

The Origins

History of Kalaripayattu

From mythological roots to global recognition — the story of the world's oldest martial art.

Mythological Origins

Created by Parashurama, who learned from Lord Shiva to protect the land of Kerala. "Kalari" means training hall; "Payattu" means combat practice.

3rd Century BCE

Historical roots traced to this period. Flourished under the patronage of local rulers during the Chera and Zamorin dynasties in Kerala.

12th–16th Century — Golden Age

Reached its zenith. Legendary warriors — Chekavars — served kings in famous Ankam (ritual duels), where single combat decided battles.

18th–19th Century — Colonial Suppression

Banned by the British. Survived in secret, passed down from Gurukkal to student in hidden training grounds, preserved by unwavering devotion.

20th Century — Revival

Revived by dedicated masters. Today Kalaripayattu is recognized globally as both an ancient martial system and a complete path of physical and spiritual wellness.
